Design & Engineering for Making (DE4M) Lab

My research in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) focuses on integrating tangible user interfaces, wearable technology, and artificial intelligence into everyday human experiences. I develop interactive systems that bridge physical and digital interactions.

I specialize in prototyping hardware-software systems, sensor integration, and AI applications for interactive experiences. My methodology combines qualitative approaches (interviews, observational studies) with quantitative techniques (sensor data analysis, surveys, experimental evaluation) through lab and field studies.

I am advised by Prof. Liang He at the DE4M Lab, UTD.

Selected Projects

Ongoing Hardware

Brain-Controlled Exoskeleton for Relearning Functional Hand Skills

Developing a brain signal simulator and designing a hand exoskeleton for Spinal Cord Injury patients, bridging high-fidelity neural decoding with adaptive wearable assistance.

TEI '26 Food HCI

PufFab: Shape Transformable Rice Paper for Playful Food Fabrication

An interactive design-to-fabrication pipeline utilizing Vietnamese rice paper for customizable edible shape transformation via modified 3D printer hardware.

In Submission AI + Writing

Human-AI Co-Writing in Knowledge-Intensive Tasks

A within-subject user study with 23 participants evaluating LLM-human collaboration patterns, analyzing how personal intentions shape the use of AI in knowledge work.

IDC '25 TUI

WooGu: Enhancing Children's Understanding of Farming through TUI

An educational system integrating digital devices, tangible user interfaces, and embodied interactions with 3D-printed farming tools and a watchOS app to teach agricultural literacy.

In Submission Accessibility

Adaptive Gaming Controller for People with Upper Limb Motor Disabilities

DIY accessible game controllers emphasizing user participation, open-source tools, and narrative inquiry methods for capturing lived experiences of users with disabilities.

Social Impact Wearable

WordLink Hub: Empowering Floating Children Through Interactive Learning

An educational system for migrant children combining WordBuddy (a wearable vocabulary device) and StorySpot Corner (an interactive library installation) for Weilan Libraries.

IoT ML

SIGHMY: Transforming Office Sighs into Positive Energy

A cactus-shaped smart device that detects workplace sighs using ML audio processing (90.8% accuracy) and responds with subtle movements, paired with an employer dashboard.

TUI + GUI Hardware

Sustainable House: Renewable Energy Interactive Model

A zero-energy house model demonstrating renewable energy integration through a combined GUI and physical model with wind turbine, solar panels, and heat pump visualizations.
