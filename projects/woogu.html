"I Want to Use Technology to Help Farmers in the Future": Enhancing Children's Understanding of Farming through TUI and Embodied Interaction

Venue
IDC '25 (Full Paper)
Duration
May 2023 – Sep 2024
Role
First Author
Advisors
Xin Tong, Liang He, Min Fan

Abstract

WooGu is an educational system integrating digital devices, Tangible User Interfaces (TUIs), and embodied interactions to teach children about the full farming process. The system combines real-time digital feedback with hands-on physical activities using 3D-printed farming tools and a watchOS app.

System Design

The system bridges 3D-printed tools with integrated sensors (IMU — accelerometers and gyroscopes) to provide multi-modal feedback (visual, haptic, and digital), encouraging children's physical engagement with farming concepts. A Recurrent Neural Network handles real-time movement classification through the watchOS companion app.

Key Findings

  • Sequence Card Sorting scores: rice pre-test 2.9 → post-test 3.9
  • Seed Status Matching accuracy: 67% → 89%
  • Knowledge Questionnaire scores: 1.53 → 5.73
  • High engagement and attitude shift toward appreciation of farming and agricultural technology
