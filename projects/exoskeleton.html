From Intention to Action: A Brain-Controlled Exoskeleton Pipeline for Relearning Functional Hand Skills

Status
Ongoing
Duration
Oct 2025 – Present
Role
Lead Researcher
Lab
DE4M Lab, UTD

Overview

This project develops a brain-controlled exoskeleton pipeline specifically designed for Spinal Cord Injury (SCI) patients to assist with Activities of Daily Living (ADLs). The work bridges high-fidelity neural decoding with adaptive wearable assistance.

Research Contributions

  • Developing a brain signal simulator based on current literature to model neural intentions for motor control
  • Designing and fabricating a hand exoskeleton specifically for SCI patients
  • Establishing a pipeline to bridge high-fidelity neural decoding with adaptive wearable assistance
